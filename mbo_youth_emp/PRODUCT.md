# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary beneficiaries** — Mbo youth (students, young entrepreneurs, trainees) browsing funding schemes, applying for scholarships/grants/empowerment programmes, and tracking their application status. They access the portal primarily via mobile phones, often on limited bandwidth.

**Administrators & verifiers** — Local government staff and trust officials who publish schemes, review applications, make approval/rejection decisions, and manage the funding cycle. They use desktop and mobile.

Both groups are equally critical. The portal must serve youth with clarity and dignity while giving administrators efficient, transparent tools.

## Product Purpose

A local-government youth funding portal for the Royal Mbo Host Community Development Trust. Admins publish scholarship, grant, and empowerment schemes; students browse and apply; the system auto-runs eligibility checks and flags conflicts; verifiers review and approve/reject. The core job: connect Mbo youth to funding opportunities through a fair, transparent, accountable process.

Success means: every eligible youth can discover and apply for funding without friction; every application is tracked transparently; administrators can manage the full cycle efficiently.

## Brand Personality

**Trustworthy, empowering, transparent** — The portal is an instrument of government trust. It must feel reliable, uplifting, and open. Nothing hidden, nothing confusing.

**Modern, efficient, professional** — Clean, fast, no-nonsense. Institutional without being bureaucratic. Youth should feel the system works *for* them, not against them.

**Community, hopeful, accessible** — Rooted in the Mbo community. Warm without being folksy. Welcoming to all youth regardless of education level or tech literacy.

## Anti-references

- **Flashy fintech / startup aesthetic** — No gradient-heavy hero sections, neon accents, over-designed cards, or "disruptive" energy. This is government infrastructure, not a VC-backed app.
- **Cold / corporate enterprise software** — No sterile white-on-white dashboards, no impersonal data-table sprawl, no "Oracle forms" energy. The portal serves real people in a community context.
- **Generic SaaS admin template** — No blue-sidebar + white-card cookie-cutter dashboards. The admin experience should feel purpose-built for this domain.
- **Nigerian government portal clichés** — No cluttered layouts, outdated typography, slow-loading pages, or bureaucratic maze navigation.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every screen should be immediately understandable. Labels are plain language. Statuses are visible. Actions are obvious. No hidden affordances.

2. **Dignity for the beneficiary** — The youth experience should feel respectful and hopeful, not like begging for assistance. Warmth without condescension.

3. **Transparency as structure** — Application status, eligibility checks, and decisions are surfaced clearly. The user always knows where they stand and why.

4. **Efficiency for the administrator** — Review queues, scheme management, and audit tools should minimize clicks and cognitive load. Dense information, cleanly presented.

5. **Accessible by default** — Mobile-first, low-bandwidth tolerant, WCAG 2.1 AA compliant. The portal works for youth on a 3G connection with a modest phone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** minimum across all surfaces
- **Mobile-first** — primary access is via mobile phones; all flows must work on small screens
- **Low-bandwidth tolerant** — minimal asset weight, no auto-play video, progressive loading
- **Literacy-inclusive** — clear plain-language labels, visual status indicators, minimal jargon
- **Reduced motion** respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`
